## What are network ports?

> Used for data input and output, directing what I want to receive and what can come in.
> Control over what data enters and what leaves.

### Each protocol generally uses a range of ports, or specific ports.

> Ports range from 0 to 65,535 and are divided into two groups:
- **Low Ports** – Well Known Ports → Protocols and Services → range 0 to 1023
- **High Ports** – Registered Ports → Responses to client requests → range 1024 to 65535

### RFC / Communication Ports*

> Most common ports:
- 20/21 → ftp
- 22 → ssh
- 23 → telnet
- 25/587 → smtp
- 53 → dns
- 67 → dhcp
- 69 → tftp
- 79 → finger
- 80 → http
- 110 → pop
- 123 → ntp
- 143 → imap
- 161/163 → snmp
- 443 → https
